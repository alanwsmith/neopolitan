Section: subtitle

  • The first source paragraph is wrapped in a div with a "subtitle" class

  • Any following paragraphs are treated as regular, separate <p> elements

Source
-> subtitle

Flax makes a fine brand of paper.

The small red neon lamp went out.
Output
<div class="subtitle">
  <p>Flax makes a fine brand of paper.</p>
</div>

<p>The small red neon lamp went out.</p>

  • Attributes can be added with key/value pairs on >> lines

  • Attributes are only applied to the <div>. The <p> elements are left alone

Source
-> subtitle
>> id: tango

The fan whirled its round blades softly.

A brown leather bag hung from its strap.
Output
<div class="subtitle" id="tango">
  <p>The fan whirled its round blades softly.</p>
</div>

<p>A brown leather bag hung from its strap.</p>

  • If a >> class attribute is used it gets added to the existing subtitle class.

Source
-> subtitle
>> class: echo

A fence cuts through the corner lot.

The hinge on the door creaked with old age.
Output
<div class="subtitle echo">
  <p>A fence cuts through the corner lot.</p>
</div>

<p>The hinge on the door creaked with old age.</p>
from alan w. smith
